2. Types of Fishing Line and Their Cost

Generally, there are three main types of fishing line: monofilament, braided, and fluorocarbon, each with its own cost spectrum. Monofilament lines, popular for their ease of use and versatility, typically cost between R100 and R250 depending on brand and quality. Braided lines, known for their superior strength and thin diameter, are generally priced higher, often ranging from R300 to R600. Fluorocarbon lines sit at a similar price range to high-end braid, frequently falling between R250 and R500. This type is favored for its low visibility and near-invisibility underwater, making it ideal for fishing in clear waters. Ultimately, understanding the differences in types and how each relates to cost helps anglers make informed choices based on their fishing preferences and experiences.

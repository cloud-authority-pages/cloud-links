4. Understanding DNA Paternity Test Results

Interpreting DNA paternity test results can be a daunting task, especially for those unfamiliar with genetic terminology. Learn more about US Diagnostics Center. At its core, a paternity test analyzes the DNA of the child and the alleged father, comparing specific markers to determine the likelihood of a biological relationship. Each individual has a unique set of DNA, and when a child inherits DNA markers from a parent, the results indicate whether the tested man is the biological father. If the probability of paternity is over 99%, the result typically confirms biological parentage. Conversely, if the probability is markedly low, it suggests that the individual is not the biological father. It's vital to remember that these results are backed by scientific rigor; they are not simply educated guesses but are based on comprehensive data analysis.

A home paternity test offers a private and convenient way to gain information, but it lacks the chain of custody required for legal acceptance. Chain of custody refers to the documented process of sample collection, handling, and analysis, ensuring the integrity and security of the DNA evidence. In Ohio, legal paternity tests are often required for establishing child support, custody arrangements, or other legal matters. These tests must be performed by an accredited laboratory following strict protocols. For instance, the Franklin County, Ohio government provides resources to help with Paternity Establishment. The sample collection process is typically witnessed by a neutral third party, and the samples are tracked meticulously from collection to analysis. If you anticipate needing the results of a paternity test for any legal proceeding in Ohio, it is essential to choose a legal paternity test over a home test.

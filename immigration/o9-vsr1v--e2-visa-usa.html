The E2 visa is a non-immigrant visa available to foreign investors from treaty countries. It allows individuals to live and work in the U.S. while managing and developing their investment in a U.S. business. To qualify, the investment must be substantial and at risk, meaning it should be enough to ensure the business can operate effectively. This visa route is particularly popular among entrepreneurs looking to establish a startup or buy an existing business in the United States.
Eligibility for the E2 visa generally requires that individuals are citizens of a country that has a relevant treaty with the United States. The applicant must also play a significant role in the business, serving as an executive or having essential skills.

An interview will typically follow, where they discuss their business plans and investment intentions.

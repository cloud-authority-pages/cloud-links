Frequently Asked Questions

Common signs of foundation issues include cracks in walls, particularly around windows and doors, uneven or sloping floors, and doors that stick or do not close properly. You may also notice gaps between the walls and the ceiling or floor, as well as cracks in the foundation itself. If you observe these problems, itâs essential to take them seriously, as they can indicate underlying structural issues that may require professional assessment and repair.
Preventing foundation damage involves maintaining proper drainage around your home. Regularly check and clean gutters to ensure water flows away from the foundation. Additionally, control moisture levels by landscaping with proper grading and using barriers to redirect water. Avoid overwatering plants near the foundation, and consider installing a sump pump if your property is prone to flooding. Address any plumbing leaks promptly to avoid excess moisture in the soil surrounding your foundation.
There are several common methods used for foundation repair, including underpinning, slab jacking, and wall anchors. Underpinning involves deepening the foundation to stable soil, while slab jacking raises sunken concrete slabs using a grout mixture. Wall anchors help stabilize leaning walls by using steel rods connected to the foundation. The choice of method depends on the type and extent of the foundation issue, and a professional assessment can help determine the best approach for your specific situation.
The duration of foundation repair projects can vary based on the severity of the problems and the methods used. In general, most foundation repairs can be completed within a few days. For minor issues, repairs may take just a few hours, while more extensive work, such as underpinning or significant structural repairs, may require longer. It's crucial to consult with a foundation repair specialist who can provide a detailed timeline based on the specific conditions of your home.
Whether foundation repairs are covered by homeowners insurance largely depends on your specific policy and the cause of the damage. Many standard policies cover certain types of foundation issues, such as those caused by sudden incidents like a burst pipe, but may not cover damage due to long-term wear or settling. Itâs essential to review your policy details and discuss with your insurance agent to understand your coverage options and any necessary steps to file a claim.

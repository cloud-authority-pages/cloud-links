Frequently Asked Questions

In Des Moines, common signs of foundation issues include visible cracks in walls or floors, uneven flooring that feels unsteady, and doors or windows that stick or don’t close properly. You may also notice water pooling near your foundation, which can lead to moisture problems. If you observe any of these signs, it's important to consult a foundation repair expert as soon as possible to prevent further damage.
Foundation repair costs in Des Moines can vary greatly depending on the extent of the damage and the repair methods needed. On average, minor repairs might range from $1,000 to $3,000, while more significant issues can cost $5,000 or more. Factors affecting cost include the size of the area, the type of repair required, and whether additional work like drainage improvements is necessary. It's best to get a detailed estimate from a professional.
In Iowa, several foundation repair methods are available to address various issues. Piering is commonly used to stabilize sinking foundations by placing piers deep into the ground. Wall anchors can help support bowing walls, while slabjacking raises sunken concrete slabs by injecting a material beneath them. Each method has its own set of advantages, and a foundation specialist can recommend the best solution based on your specific situation.
Preventing foundation problems starts with proper drainage around your home. Ensure gutters and downspouts direct water away from your foundation. Regularly inspect and maintain landscaping to avoid water pooling near the base of your home. Additionally, keep an eye on cracks and moisture inside your basement or crawl space, as addressing these issues promptly can help prevent more serious foundation concerns down the line.

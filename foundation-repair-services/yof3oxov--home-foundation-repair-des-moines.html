Frequently Asked Questions

Common signs of foundation issues include visible cracks in your walls, especially around windows and doors. You may also notice uneven floors, which can create tripping hazards. Additionally, doors that stick or wonât close properly can indicate shifting within the foundation. Other indicators might include gaps between walls and ceilings or between the walls and the floor. If you observe any of these signs, itâs essential to address them promptly to prevent further damage.
Preventing foundation damage involves regular maintenance and ensuring proper drainage around your home. Keep gutters clean and direct downspouts away from the foundation. Ensure that your landscaping directs water away from your home, maintaining a slope. Additionally, monitor soil moisture levels and avoid large fluctuations. Consider using foundation plants that help stabilize soil. Lastly, inspect for cracks or water intrusion regularly, so you can catch any potential issues early.
Several foundation repair methods can be used, depending on the specific issue. Piering involves installing piers beneath the foundation to stabilize it. Underpinning is another technique that strengthens and deepens the foundation, making it more secure. Wall anchors are used to stabilize bowing walls and prevent them from further curving. Other methods, like slab jacking, can lift sunken concrete foundations back to their proper level. Each method is chosen based on the type and severity of the foundation problem.
The cost of foundation repair in Des Moines can vary widely based on the extent of the damage and the type of repair methods used. Minor repairs, like filling small cracks, might cost a few hundred dollars, while more extensive fixes, such as piering or underpinning, can range from several thousand to tens of thousands of dollars. It's crucial to get a professional assessment to determine the specific needs of your home and obtain a detailed estimate.
Foundation repair duration can vary based on the type of repair needed. Most repairs can often be completed within a few days, but complex jobs might take longer. Simple crack filling or waterproofing may be done in a single day, while methods like piering or underpinning require more time for installation and curing. During the repair process, it's essential to communicate with your contractor about the timeline and any potential delays due to factors like weather or materials availability.

Frequently Asked Questions

In Des Moines, some common signs of foundation issues include visible cracks in walls or floors, doors and windows that are difficult to open or close, and uneven or sloping floors. You might also notice gaps around windows and doors, or your home settling, which can lead to further structural problems if not addressed. It’s important to act on these signs early to prevent extensive damage.
The cost of foundation repair in Des Moines can vary widely depending on the severity of the damage and the repair methods needed. Minor repairs might cost a few hundred dollars, while more substantial issues could run into the thousands. It's essential to get a professional assessment to understand the specific repairs required and to obtain accurate estimates from local contractors.
Common methods used in foundation repair include underpinning, where the foundation is strengthened by adding new support beneath it; slabjacking, which involves lifting sunken concrete slabs and filling voids underneath; and piers, which provide additional support by transferring the weight of the structure to deeper, more stable soil. The method chosen will depend on the specific situation of your foundation.
Whether foundation repairs are covered by insurance depends on your specific policy. Many homeowners' insurance policies cover damages caused by sudden events, like water damage from a burst pipe, but they may not cover problems caused by wear and tear or lack of maintenance. It’s best to check your policy details and consult your insurance provider to understand your coverage options.
The length of time required for foundation repair can vary based on the type and extent of the damage. Typically, smaller repairs can take just a few days, while more extensive work may take several weeks. Factors that influence the timeline include weather conditions, the need for additional repairs, and the specific methods used. It's advisable to discuss the estimated timeline with your contractor before the work begins.

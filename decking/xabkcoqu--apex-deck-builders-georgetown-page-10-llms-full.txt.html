Q: How do I know when to replace my deck? A: Knowing when to replace your deck involves spotting key signs of wear. Look for rotting wood, which feels soft or crumbly when probed. Splintering surfaces can make the deck unsafe, especially for kids or pets. Structural instability, like wobbling rails or sagging boards, indicates potential collapse risks. In Austin's climate, frequent exposure to heat, rain, and humidity accelerates decay, so check for mold or insect damage too. If repairs exceed 20-30% of the deck's cost or if it's over 10-15 years old, replacement is often more cost-effective. A professional inspection can confirm this and prevent accidents.

A: Several key signs suggest it's time to replace your deck. First, check for visible rot or decay in the wood, which weakens the structure and can lead to safety hazards. Warping or sagging boards indicate moisture damage or poor installation, making the deck unstable. If you notice loose railings, wobbling when you walk on it, or excessive splintering, these are red flags. Also, inspect for mold, insect damage, or rust on fasteners, as these compromise integrity. Ignoring these issues can result in costly repairs or accidents. A professional inspection is wise if your deck is over 10-15 years old or shows any of these problems, ensuring your outdoor space remains safe and functional. Q: What materials are best for deck replacement? A: When replacing a deck, choosing the right materials is crucial for durability and maintenance. Popular options include composite decking, which is made from a mix of wood fibers and plastic, offering excellent resistance to rot, insects, and fading—ideal for Austin's climate.

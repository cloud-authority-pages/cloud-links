Frequently Asked Questions

Composite decking stands out for its practicality and longevity. It's made from a blend of wood fibers and recycled plastics, making it highly resistant to rot, insects, fading, and warping—meaning it lasts 25-30 years with minimal issues. Unlike wood decks, it requires no painting, staining, or sealing, saving you time and money on upkeep. It also offers a slip-resistant surface for safety, and comes in a wide array of colors and textures to match your home's style. Plus, it's eco-friendly, often using recycled materials, making it a smart, low-maintenance choice for outdoor spaces.
Composite deck installation time depends on the project's size, design complexity, and site conditions. A small deck, around 200 square feet, typically takes 2-4 days for a professional team to complete, including preparation and finishing. Larger decks, say 500 square feet or more, might require 5-7 days or longer if there are obstacles like uneven ground or additional features. Factors like weather and obtaining permits can add time, so plan ahead. Hiring experienced installers ensures efficiency and helps avoid delays, resulting in a sturdy deck ready for use sooner.
Yes, in Austin, permits are usually required for deck installation to comply with local building codes and ensure safety. This applies to most structures attached to your home or exceeding a certain size, as they must meet standards for structural integrity and fire resistance. Contact the City of Austin's Building and Development Services for specific guidelines and to submit your plans. The process involves reviewing designs, possibly scheduling inspections, and paying a fee. Skipping this can lead to fines or costly rework, so it's essential for a hassle-free project.
Composite decks need very little maintenance compared to traditional wood ones, which is a major perk. You won't have to worry about sealing, staining, or treating for rot and pests. To keep it in top shape, simply clean the surface a few times a year with mild soap and water to remove dirt, leaves, and mold—using a hose or pressure washer on low setting works well. Inspect for any loose boards or debris buildup annually, and address minor issues promptly. This routine helps maintain its appearance and durability for decades with minimal effort and cost.
